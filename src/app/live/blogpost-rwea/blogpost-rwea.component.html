An Unusual Evening in Alpine

We struck up a conversation with the bartender...

...in the Hotel Paisano in Marfa, Texas. He told us that while he works in Marfa, he actually lives in Alpine, about 20 miles east on highway 90 and since we were staying in the Lost Alaskan RV park in Alpine, we asked him to recommend some cool, local places to get a drink. He said we should try either Harry's Tenaja or the Hotel Ritchey.

A few nights later we found ourselves going into the Hotel Ritchey, once a hotel but now a wine saloon and beer garden, where I ordered a sparkling water and the wife ordered a glass of Spanish red. The main room in the bar has about 6 small tables so we picked one out and sat down.

“Austin may be weird, but Alpine is far out"

unofficial slogan of Alpine, Texas

“The Murphys, who were Irish Catholics, officially filed for and developed the plat for the original town of Murphyville. In 1888, the village name was changed to Alpine.”

-B.J. Gallego (from La Vista de la Frontera Vol. 16(1):10.)

The bar looks a lot like the Hollywood image of an old West bar...

...in fact we were commenting on that when Roberta, the woman at the next table began to explain the history of the hotel to us, which you can read here. Within a few minutes we were joined by Albert, a scruffy, mildly inebriate character who I assumed was the town drunk. He asked Roberta if he could sit at her table and because she acquiesced so begrudgingly I expected him to be one of those annoying drunks who gets on everyone's nerves.

Upon seeing Albert join us, a man named Ted, a temporary Chicago transplant, pulled up a chair.

We chatted about the virtues of Alpine for awhile...

...such as the climate, the restaurants and the people. At some point, someone mentioned the Marfa lights and the term "weird shit" was used and that seemed to be the cue Ted was waiting for and he said "Speaking of weird shit, Albert here is the local expert on weird shit!". As I began to speculate that he probably worked at the local waste treatment plant, Albert began to expound on his favorite topic: "Did Indigenous Peoples in the area feed mushrooms to their domesticated turkey's, especially during hard times?". Determining this involved, among other things, a study of fossilized turkey shit, hence the reference to his expertise.


I was shocked...I told Albert "When you walked up I thought you would have nothing interesting to say", talk about a lesson in not judging a book by it's cover!


Anyway, Albert began to heat up to his topic, in that mildly drunk speed-talking way. He got especially excited when he found out that Eloisa was from Spain. He described how he was researching colonial Spanish papers looking for clues in his search. He explained how there were 3 words in colonial Spanish that were used for "mushroom" and he wondered if she could elaborate on the differences between the words. After Eloisa did her best to field his question she finally asked him why he cared about this subject and that's when we learned that Albert taught at Sul Ross University and was on a research grant. He admitted there was no obvious practical application for learning the answer to the turkey shit question, except to gain knowledge for knowledge's sake.

After chatting for awhile we got hungry so we decided to go next door to the Mexican restaurant. As we got up to leave, Roberta also made her her escape, it was obvious she'd heard this shit before.
